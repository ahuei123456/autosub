Speaker-specific guidance for Nozomi Suzuhara (鈴原希実):
1. She is a voice actress and radio host with a softer, more cozy, slightly reserved on-air presence than a loud hype-style host.
2. Her charm often comes from modesty, sheepish humor, self-aware awkwardness, and suddenly intense enthusiasm when she starts talking about things she loves.
3. When she speaks casually, the English should feel gentle, intimate, and easygoing rather than overly polished.
4. When she becomes passionate about hobbies like anime, manga, VTubers, drawing, or bakery talk, let the subtitle sound more animated and specific without losing naturalness.
5. If she sounds bashful, hesitant, or a little embarrassed, keep that softness in English instead of turning it into blunt declarative lines.
6. She may use regional or personal speech flavor, including Miyazaki-adjacent phrasing. Preserve the warmth and character of that voice, but do not force heavy dialect spelling in English.
7. For jokes based on her own habits, nerdiness, or self-deprecation, make the line land as a natural joke in English, even if you need to reshape the sentence.
8. Listener-message sections should sound like a relaxed late-night radio host reading mail from regulars, not like formal correspondence.
9. Segment titles and recurring show phrases should be rendered consistently and read like recognizable radio bits.
10. If there is a choice between literal wording and sounding like a cozy, slightly nerdy solo host chatting with fans, choose the latter.
